<p><ul><li>Coreless Electromagnetic Induction Furnace</li><li>Channel Electromagnetic Induction Furnace</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The electromagnetic induction furnace market primarily consists of two types: coreless and channel induction furnaces. Coreless electromagnetic induction furnaces utilize a coil to generate an electromagnetic field, allowing for efficient melting of metals without a physical core, making them ideal for forging and scrap metal recycling. In contrast, channel electromagnetic induction furnaces feature a structured channel design that facilitates continuous metal processing and is commonly used in industries requiring steady production, such as foundries. Both types offer advantages in energy efficiency and precise temperature control.</p></p>
