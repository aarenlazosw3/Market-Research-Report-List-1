<p><p>The hemp fiber market can be divided into two primary types: long (bast) fibers and short (core) fibers. Long fibers, derived from the outer stem of the hemp plant, are prized for their strength and durability, making them ideal for textiles and composite materials. Short fibers, sourced from the inner core, are softer and used in products like paper, insulation, and biodegradable plastics. Each type caters to different industries, driving demand for sustainable and eco-friendly materials.</p></p>
