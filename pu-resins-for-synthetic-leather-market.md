<p><p>PU resins, or polyurethane resins, are essential in the production of synthetic leather, which can be classified into two main market types: dry-process and wet-process synthetic leather. Dry-process synthetic leather typically involves the application of PU resin onto a substrate without the need for water, resulting in a thicker, more durable product. In contrast, wet-process synthetic leather uses water-based solutions, allowing for finer textures and enhanced breathability. Both types offer unique benefits, catering to diverse consumer preferences and applications.</p></p>
