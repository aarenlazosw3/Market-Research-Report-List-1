<p><p>The methyl methacrylate market is segmented based on production methods: the ACH method, isobutylene method, and ethylene method. The ACH method involves the reaction of acetone cyanohydrin, yielding high-purity methyl methacrylate. The isobutylene method utilizes isobutylene and methacrylic acid, offering efficient production with lower costs. Lastly, the ethylene method involves ethylene in its production, focusing on sustainability. Each method has its advantages, influencing market dynamics, pricing, and application across industries such as automotive, construction, and electronics.</p></p>
